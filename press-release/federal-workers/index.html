From Layoffs to Little Forests: Planting Hope for Public Servants

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

Los Angeles, CA - 02/18/2025 — For Every Star A Tree presents Seeds of Support for Federal Workers, a transformative community Planting Day that celebrates resilience, faith, and the enduring spirit in the face of the USA's ongoing government layoffs.

Taking place on February 28th in East Lancaster, this event is a call to action for volunteers, families, and organizations to join hands in planting a fire-resistant, sustainable future.

SEEDS OF SUPPORT aims to heal both the environment and the community by planting native trees, plants, and wildlife habitat gardens to support pollinators, enrich biodiversity, and encourage climate resilience.

We will center impacted federal workers while offering hands-on educational workshops about wildfire recovery and sustainable gardening at the nonprofit ecofarm's quarter-acre Butterfly Habitat. By bringing people together to plant seeds of hope, SEEDS OF SUPPORT fosters connection and collective action in the face of government shutdown.

A Day of Action for Federal Workers

Departments like the USDA, NRCS, FSA, and countless others have long since helped farms like For Every Star A Tree. When layoffs began sweeping through each layer of government, they left more than empty seats—they ignited a passion to regrow and renew what was lost.

"It doesn't matter who is in office. The Earth still needs us," said Billimarie Lubiano Robinson, founder of the nonprofit ecofarm. "Now is the time to plant gardens, tiny forests, and pollinator habitats for the next generation to come. Those that have been impacted by the government layoffs can choose to come together to grow a greener future."

SEEDS OF SUPPORT isn't just about planting—it's about cultivating hope. This event will feature:

  • Planting Activities: Volunteers will help restore desertified land by planting drought-tolerant, fire-resistant native species.
  • Educational Workshops: Learn about wildfire recovery, sustainable gardening, and how to support local ecosystems.
  • Family-Friendly Fun: People of all ages are invited to sow seeds, water plants, and play in the Butterfly Forest.

About For Every Star A Tree

For Every Star A Tree is on a mission to cultivate abandonded, desertified land into regenerative habitats.

The nonprofit inspires collective action to create a healthier planet. Every donation helps fund essential resources like water, seeds, saplings, and tools, ensuring the success of community planting projects like SEEDS OF SUPPORT.

Want to get involved? You can volunteer, donate, or share the event at the SEEDS OF SUPPORT event page.

To support the nonprofit's mission to grow a greener, breathable, livable tomorrow, you can give a tax-deductible donation.

How You Can Help

  • Volunteer: Roll up your sleeves and join us on planting day.
  • Donate: Contributions will directly fund trees, seeds, tools, and event logistics.
  • Spread the Word: Share the event with your community and bring friends and family.

"The United States has always called itself the land of dreams," said Robinson, who represents the For Every Star A Tree collective. SEEDS OF SUPPORT is "our collective expression of transforming a challenge into an opportunity."

Everyone is invited to help us grow Los Angeles county into a breathable, livable, greener home.
