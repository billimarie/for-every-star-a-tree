How Do You Know You Can Pull This Off?

We’ve been diligently working on this concept for the past two years.

By working, I mean planting. Watering. Fighting against pests. Learning the conditions of each season. Coming up with our own hacks and learning from our desert neighbors what works and what doesn’t throughout decades of experience.

You can say we earned the right to claim this dream through our own blood, sweat, tears…and sunburns.

We’ve reached a point where we know what trees will survive, where to plant, and the right permaculture methodologies to use.

Okay…But Why Do YOU Want To Plant A Forest In The Desert?

Desert regreening projects are not a new phenomenon. I was personally inspired by something a friend said to me while we were walking in the remote woods of Sweden one winter day:

“We live in a man-made woods.”

Being from the suburbs of Los Angeles, it had never occurred to me that a forest could be planted.

That epiphany happened seven years ago. It has echoed in my head ever since.

October 2022

Fast-forward to 2020. After the birth of my daughter, the death of my father, and the start of COVID lockdowns–all in that fateful March–I started to shift my focus toward “healing abandoned land.”

That metaphor anchored me through grief and trauma during an uncertain time. Imagine not being able to have family and friends over for a funeral.

Or being banned from celebrating the arrival of a new life in person.

Then imagine staying inside, protecting that newborn all day and all night, while an unknown virus takes root.

For reasons beyond me, that death and that birth inspired me to recontextualize the degraded desert lands in our valley community as “abandoned.”

I researched by reading books, watching YouTube videos, and visiting different forums that encouraged desert regreening projects.

I followed experts in soil, ecosystems, and waterways. I even enrolled in a Cornell University Small Farms program and presented my “Desert Forest” concept that fall.

October 2022

In 2021, I was able to finance the purchase of a raw, desert 10-acre parcel on my birthday.

To be clear: I’m not a gardener. I’m not a farming expert.

I’m just an average person who really likes working with the land.

And I’ve come to believe the future depends on people like you and like me. The non-experts. The people who just decide one day to quietly plant, and water, and plant and water, again.

I'm ready

Alright, I’m In. What Exactly Do You Need?

This crowdfunding project is years in the making. I, along with my friends and family, have been planting trees on a 10-acre desert parcel since 2021.

We gathered together because we felt it was important to do something real, something concrete, to increase the world’s forest canopy–even if our little, small actions are just a drop in a very large ocean.

December 2021

We’ve come a long way since our first tree in 2021. Now that we’ve figured out how to protect each tree from wind, heat, and pests, we’re ready to invite you to join our Desert Forest dream.

Our initial goal is to raise $4,000 to install an automatic drip irrigation system. This will line the perimeter of a 100ft2 space next to our tiny house (the Starry Night Skoolie). The remaining funds will help prepare the ground, purchase plants, and cover the cost of labor for planting the new garden.

We will install this water system and plant additional trees the week of October 7th–in the middle of our crowdfunding campaign–until October 21st.

It's go time

What If You Raise More Than $4,000?

I’ve calculated the costs of several stretch goals if we are lucky enough to raise more than our original goal.

Our Stretch Goals

For $10,000: we will be able to plant a 300ft2 garden, which we will lovingly name after one of my favorite video games as a kid: Kirby’s Whispy Woods.

For $18,000: we will be able to add additional solar panels to our tiny house–the Starry Night Skoolie–which will help us make a refuge where we can blast the air conditioner after a long day planting in 110º weather.

For $24,000: we will be able to expand our Desert Forest into a 600ft2 area, which will also be named after another favorite video game: Legend of Zelda’s Kokiri Forest.

For $30,000: we will be able to purchase a 10,000 gallon water tank. This will go a long way to creating a sustainable ecosystem for the trees.

Last but not least, if we reach $50,000: we will be able to expand our Desert Forest into a 1,500ft2 area, which will be named after an all-time favorite video game: Final Fantasy Tactics’ Araguay Woods. (I played this game so much that I had dreams about this chessboard of a forest as a kid.)

How Will The Rewards Work?

Unlike other crowdfunding campaigns, all Backers will receive my Google Voice number so that you can text and call me with questions and regular updates about the Desert Forest dream.

Why? Because the beauty of crowdsourcing--of crowdfunding--is in the evolution of a project from one creator’s hands…into a global community’s arms.

We have a host of Flash Rewards, and Stretch Goal Rewards. But we're starting off simple:

  • $25 for a remote ticket to our Post-Campaign Stargazing Party
  • $50 for an in-person ticket to our Post-Campaign Stargazing Party
  • $100 to have a tree planted in your honor, where we go live so you and your friends can watch it happen in real-time
