our mission

Heal land, heal ourselves

We all know Earth is interconnected.

Degraded land in Southern California is going to affect you--and the global climate--wherever you are in the world.

It's our mission to heal as much abandoned land in Antelope Valley through optimistic rewilding, compassionate earthcare, and dedicated community work.

A direct community-led response to climate change and soil erosion, For Every Star, A Tree remains steadfast in its stewardship and aspires to cultivate verdant fields, gardens, and forests for all species to enjoy. We are committed to sustainability and the global community at large.

Together, we can grow this special land into a reawakened oasis.

our values

Restoring degraded land

A hopeful, global effort for the next generation.

Considered an affordable but desolate part of Los Angeles county, the Antelope Valley has a history of repeated land degradation and soil erosion by well-meaning but damaging farming practices.

Dust storms, drought, and high winds are a common feature of this valley because there is a severe lack of grasslands–which is what it historically used to be, back when there were still antelopes in the Antelope Valley.

Our values stem from my happy childhood memories of playing in small but verdant yards. My mother, an expert farmer and gardener, transformed our childhood home in the concrete suburbs of Los Angeles by cultivating a wild garden in our front yard. I called this garden “the jungle,” and often gathered ripe berries and guava for fun.

I also remember playing tag with my best friends in the neighborhood. Whenever I would hide, I’d choose this small green patch in front of a neighbor’s front yard. Besides our house, it was the only other front yard on the block that had a shaded understory from the large pine trees that grew there.

I called this magical place “the forest”.

This innocent, childlike appreciation for green spaces–no matter how big or how small–is a core value of our organization. We hope to offer this beauty to others.

“For Every Star, A Tree” heals abandoned land by cultivating little pocket forests and gardens, and offers these creatively-inspired sanctuary spaces to others who are seeking solace from grief, illness, or trauma. We are compassionate caretakers of earth and the human spirit.

our vision

10 Acres, 10 Years

We are rooted and committed to this land.

Our vision is to mobilize the global community to help us steward our ten acres of desert land.

That is why we are inviting you to plant a tree with us...from anywhere in the world.

With our remote supporters, backers, and believers–along with our dedicated team of earthworkers–we believe a complete reversal of land degradation is possible.

We anticipate that within 10 years--with lots of support and earthcare--we will see a mature, established, sustainable, wild sanctuary for pollinators, birds, and humans.

We hope to show you the optimistic side of climate change--what we're all capable of when we grow, together.
