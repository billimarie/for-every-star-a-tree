Here's our plan.

This Autumn, we will plant 90 - 150 native tree saplings, shrubs, and vegetative plants within a 300ft2 garden plot in the Mojave Desert.

This style of planting is known as the Miyawaki Forest method. It is what people use to cultivate "pocket forests."

The funds raised will go directly to our nonprofit: For Every Star, A Tree.

$8,000 will cover:

  • Protective fencing to guard against pests
  • 24/7 Security camera
  • Automated drip irrigation system
  • Driving, hauling, and gardening labor
  • Plant purchases from local nurseries

Okay...but why are you personally doing this?

I was personally inspired by something a friend said to me while we were walking in the remote woods of Sweden one winter day:

“We live in a man-made woods.”

Being from the suburbs of Los Angeles, it had never occurred to me that a forest could be planted.

That epiphany happened seven years ago. It has echoed in my head ever since.

October 2022

Fast-forward to 2020. After the birth of my daughter, the death of my father, and the start of COVID lockdowns–all in that fateful March–I started to shift my focus toward “healing abandoned land.”

That metaphor anchored me through grief and trauma during an uncertain time.

Imagine not being able to have family and friends over for a funeral. Or being banned from celebrating the arrival of a new life in person.

Then imagine staying inside, protecting that newborn all day and all night, while an unknown virus takes root.

October 2022

For reasons beyond me, that death and that birth inspired me to recontextualize the degraded desert lands in our valley community as “abandoned.”

I researched by reading books, watching YouTube videos, and visiting different forums that encouraged desert regreening projects.

I followed experts in soil, permaculture, agriculture, gardening, and hydrology. I even enrolled in a Cornell University Small Farms extension program and presented my “Desert Forest” concept that fall.

In 2021, I was able to finance the purchase of a raw, desert 10-acre parcel on my birthday.

To be clear: I’m not a "master gardener." I’m not a farming expert.

I’m just an average person who really likes working with the land.

And I’ve come to believe the future depends on people like you and like me. The non-experts.

The people who one day just decide to plant a forest, and water...then plant and water, again.

Alright, I'm in. What exactly do you need?

This Desert Forest dream is years in the making. I, along with my friends and family, have been planting trees on this 10-acre desert parcel since 2021.

We gathered together because we felt it was important to do something real, something concrete, to increase the world’s forest canopy–even if our little, small actions are just a drop in a very large ocean.

December 2021

We’ve come a long way since our first tree in 2021. (A western sycamore, if you were wondering.) Now that we’ve figured out how to protect each tree from wind, heat, and pests, we’re ready to invite you to join our Desert Forest dream.

Our project this autumn is to:

  • Dig a 300ft2 garden (approximately ~3 feet deep)
  • Transport biomass, compost, mulch, woodchips, straw, and manure for healthy soil
  • Install an automated drip irrigation system, powered by the solar electricity we already have
  • Transport and plant 90 - 150 native plants that were picked to survive in this region
  • Install a protective pest-proof fence around the perimeter of the garden

Thanks to our generous advisory partner, KG Sponsors, we are halfway to our fundraising goal of $8,000. We hope to raise an additional $4,000 to see this project into fruition.
